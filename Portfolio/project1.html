COMPUTATIONAL SIMULATION OF 3D KNITWEAR

Overview

One of the critical challenges of 3D knitting is the transformation from 3D shapes to 2D textile designs. This project focused on the development of novel design tools, based on parametric CAD software, to simulate the tensile state of a tubular fabric.

Design Process: First, a physical model was created to analyze the elasticity of the fabric. Second, a digital model was designed to simulate the parameters that correctly represent the measured properties. Third,after comparing the physical fabric and digital model, a linear relationship between rest length factor and distance was discovered, and a lamp was designed using the model.

Model

Physical Model

June 2019 - July 2019

Step 1: Initial Research and Exploration

At the very beginning of the project, we learned the basic structure knowledge of circular knitting. At the same time, the properties of fabric, such as elasticity and density, were explored in order to find the influence on the products

Step 2: Build Structure Model

After receiving the fabric tube samples from cooperated company Santoni, we designed a physical structure made up of three rings to simulate the effects of fabric surface changed by the elasticity of the textile. The sample were controlled by a variable method that changed the structure and stretchability of the fabric at the same radius

Step 3: Testing Procedure

1) Place the tube on the testing structure and make sure center point, start and ending were correctly aligned

2) Take photos of the silhouette of the fabric

3) Measure the edge of the fabric on pre-determined locations

Digital Model

July 2019 - August 2019

Step 1：Build Parametric Model

After several iterations to improve the grasshopper model, the digital simulation result was almost match with the elasticity effects measured from physical model. Three main factors were found to decide the result: gravity, stiffness, and rest length factor. Among them, rest length factor was deduced to be the most decisive factor and was directly related with tensile distance

Step 2: Testing Procedure

4) Add measured points to the database

5) Compare points using Grasshopper simulation

6) Find measured points relationship to validate the model

Step 3: Discovery and Application

The regression analysis shows a clear relationship between the measured deformation and the ''rest length'' parameter of digital simulation.The experiment result of elasticity simulation is finally applied to the 3D Knitting lamp design
